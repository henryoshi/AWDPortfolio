For years I have said I will learn the guitar, learn how to sing properly, and make real art. My liberal art friends always encourage it. They understand something I do not. Or did not. All I crave now is moss. A blanket infested with mud, bugs, and rot. I was smarter in high-school, sure of myself, I am wiser now, and all the worse for it.

Complaining and harping on creativity and expression. Laughable. I chose engineering for this very reason. Correct answers, logic, the lack of emotions. It was perfect. No messy arguments, no pain or disappointment, just electrons and metal. A bird screeching for its cage to be open, yet the door is ajar; looking over the edge causes the worm in its stomach to crawl up its throat. A swiss-army-human, shaving down metal files, tirelessly scraping against screws it was never meant to fit. The screw is not all that is stripped.

"Be kind to yourself," they say. How barbaric we treat ourselves. Yet, no one else can let me down the same way. The trap of self-pity is deep, but I do not believe I fall into it. I have been given every opportunity I could ask for, I have succeeded in school, I have known love. I feel no pity, only frustration.
